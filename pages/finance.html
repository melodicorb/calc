Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate are the financial calculations?
Our financial calculators use standard financial formulas and are designed to provide accurate results. However, they should be used as a guide only and not as a substitute for professional financial advice. Actual results may vary based on specific terms and conditions of financial products.
What is the difference between simple and compound interest?
Simple interest is calculated only on the initial principal amount. Compound interest, on the other hand, is calculated on the initial principal and also on the accumulated interest of previous periods. This means that compound interest can make your money grow faster over time compared to simple interest.
How is the loan EMI calculated?
The Equated Monthly Installment (EMI) is calculated using the formula: EMI = P × r × (1 + r)^n / ((1 + r)^n - 1), where P is the principal loan amount, r is the monthly interest rate (annual rate divided by 12 and then divided by 100), and n is the loan tenure in months.
How often are the currency exchange rates updated?
Our currency converter uses fixed exchange rates for demonstration purposes. In a real-world scenario, exchange rates fluctuate constantly. For the most accurate and up-to-date exchange rates, we recommend checking with financial institutions or official currency exchange services.
Can I save my calculation results for future reference?
Yes, most of our financial calculators include options to save your calculation history locally on your device or copy results to clipboard. This allows you to refer back to previous calculations or share them with others as needed.
